# hejkohi business plan outline

This is the working structure for turning `hejkohi` from concept into a partner-ready business plan.

## 1. Executive summary

- What hejkohi is in one sentence
- Why the concept is differentiated
- What the first flagship should prove
- What kind of partner or capital is being sought

## 2. Brand and proposition

- Core brand thesis
- Emotional promise to guests
- Why Scandinavian calm and Japanese presence belong together
- What makes the concept memorable without becoming themed

Status: defined

## 3. Customer and daypart model

- Primary guest types
- Morning, midday, afternoon, and weekend use cases
- Repeat-visit logic
- Spend behavior and dwell behavior assumptions

Status: defined

## 4. Offer and menu architecture

- Core coffee offer
- Tea and non-coffee offer
- Food and bakery strategy
- Price ladder
- Seasonal or rotating items policy

Status: draft

## 5. Location strategy

- Target city
- Neighborhood profile
- Footfall quality
- Visibility requirements
- Rent guardrails
- Size and shell criteria

Status: draft

## 6. Operating model

- Opening hours
- Team structure
- Training model
- Service style
- Back-of-house needs
- Supplier and sourcing model

Status: draft

## 7. Financial model

- Opening budget
- Working capital
- Revenue scenarios
- Gross margin targets
- Labor and rent targets
- Payback expectations

Status: draft

## 8. Go-to-market and launch

- Pre-opening community strategy
- Soft launch plan
- Local partnerships
- Content and brand rollout
- First 90-day learning agenda

Status: draft

## 9. Risk and mitigation

- Site risk
- Demand risk
- Staffing risk
- Concept dilution risk
- Build-cost risk

Status: defined at principle level, needs city-specific detail

## 10. Investment and next steps

- Capital required
- Use of funds
- Partner role
- Milestones after funding
- Decision points for scaling beyond one site

Status: draft

## Immediate next work

1. Choose the target city and likely neighborhood types.
2. Build a first-location budget with real rent and fit-out assumptions.
3. Refine the menu into a practical opening menu with target margins.
4. Replace the temporary `hej` mark with a dedicated `hejkohi` identity.
5. Turn the scenario model into a full monthly P&L and cash-flow view.
